## Design Guidelines

### Visual Style:
- **Colors**: Primary #04362c (dark green), Accent #0DAD8D (mint green)
- **Typography**: Clean, modern sans-serif
- **Imagery**: Screenshots, diagrams, minimal icons
- **Layout**: Lots of white space, clear hierarchy

### Content Principles:
- **One idea per slide**
- **Visual over text** (use images, charts, diagrams)
- **Big, bold headlines**
- **Bullet points (max 5 per slide)**
- **Data and numbers** (when relevant)

### Presentation Tips:
- **Practice timing**: ~1 minute per slide
- **Tell a story**: Problem → Solution → Impact
- **Use demos**: Show the product when possible
- **Be enthusiastic**: Show passion for the product
- **Prepare for Q&A**: Anticipate questions

## Alternative: Demo-Heavy Deck (For Product Demos)

## Notes for Presenter

1. **Start strong**: Hook them in the first 30 seconds
2. **Show, don't tell**: Use screenshots and demos
3. **Tell stories**: Use examples and scenarios
4. **Be specific**: Use numbers and data
5. **End with impact**: What's the big picture?
6. **Practice transitions**: Smooth flow between slides
7. **Prepare backup**: Have answers ready for common questions

---

## Customization Options

- **For Investors**: Emphasize market size, traction, business model
- **For Users**: Focus on features, benefits, ease of use
- **For Partners**: Highlight integration, white-label, API capabilities
- **For Developers**: Deep dive into technology, architecture, APIs

Adjust content depth and focus based on your audience!
